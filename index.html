    A little history about... me.

    Hello, I'm David Gilmour. I was born on March 6, 1946, in Cambridge, England. My passion for music began at a young age, and in 1967 I joined the band Pink Floyd, shortly before Syd Barrett left. As a member of the band, I contributed guitar, vocals, and songwriting, and took part in creating albums such as The Dark Side of the Moon, Wish You Were Here, and The Wall.

    In the 1980s, after Roger Waters left, I took on the leadership of the band. Under my direction, Pink Floyd released the albums A Momentary Lapse of Reason and The Division Bell. At the same time, I developed a solo career and released several albums, including David Gilmour (1978) and About Face (1984).
